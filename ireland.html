Why Ireland?

Europe's Fastest-Growing Economy for Skilled Workers

Ireland has transformed into one of Europe's most dynamic economies. Home to the European headquarters of Google, Meta, Apple, Microsoft, and hundreds of multinationals — the demand for skilled professionals has never been higher.

With a booming construction sector, a thriving tech industry, and a healthcare system actively recruiting internationally, Ireland offers genuine career progression and a high quality of life.

  • Construction boom: €165 billion National Development Plan driving demand for engineers, project managers, and tradespeople
  • Tech hub: 9 of the world's top 10 tech companies have European HQs in Ireland
  • Healthcare: HSE actively recruiting nurses, doctors, and allied health professionals internationally
  • English-speaking: No language barrier — Ireland is the only English-speaking country in the EU
  • Gateway to Europe: EU membership means freedom of movement once you have residency

Work Permits

Understanding Irish Work Permits

Ireland has a structured employment permit system. Here's what skilled migrants need to know. Note: This is informational only — consult our partner immigration advisors for personal advice.

Most Common

Critical Skills Employment Permit

For high-demand occupations. Faster processing, leads to stamp 4 (work without a permit) after 2 years.

  • Minimum salary: €38,000/year
  • Occupation must be on the Critical Skills list
  • Processing: 4–8 weeks
  • Family can join immediately
  • Stamp 4 after 2 years

Covers: IT, engineering, healthcare, finance, construction management

General Employment Permit

For roles not on the Critical Skills list. Employer applies on your behalf. More restrictions but widely used.

  • Minimum salary: €34,000/year
  • Labour Market Needs Test required
  • Processing: 8–16 weeks
  • Family join after 12 months
  • Renewable annually

Covers: Most skilled roles not on Critical Skills list

Other Permit Types

Additional pathways depending on your situation and profession.

  • Graduate Permit — for non-EEA graduates of Irish institutions
  • Researcher Permit — for academic researchers
  • Intra-Company Transfer — for multinationals transferring staff
  • EU Blue Card — for highly qualified non-EU nationals

Regions

Where to Live in Ireland

Ireland is small but diverse. Each region has its own character, cost of living, and job market.

Dublin

Ireland's capital and economic powerhouse. Home to most multinationals and the largest job market. Higher cost of living but highest salaries.

Avg rent (1-bed): €2,000–€2,500/mo
Good public transport
Largest job market

Cork

Ireland's second city. Strong pharma, tech, and construction sectors. More affordable than Dublin with a vibrant food and culture scene.

Avg rent (1-bed): €1,400–€1,800/mo
Strong pharma sector
Great quality of life

Galway

The cultural capital of the west. Growing tech and medtech sector. Stunning scenery, vibrant arts scene, and a strong expat community.

Avg rent (1-bed): €1,200–€1,600/mo
Medtech hub
Vibrant culture

Limerick & Beyond

Limerick is Ireland's fastest-growing city. More affordable, strong construction and manufacturing sectors. Great base for exploring the Wild Atlantic Way.

Avg rent (1-bed): €1,000–€1,400/mo
Fastest growing city
Most affordable

Settling In

Your First 90 Days in Ireland

A practical checklist for getting set up quickly and confidently.

Week 1–2: Admin

  • Register with GNIB/IRP (immigration)
  • Apply for PPS Number (tax ID)
  • Open a bank account (AIB, Bank of Ireland, Revolut)
  • Register with a GP (doctor)
  • Get an Irish SIM card

Month 1: Housing

  • Use Daft.ie and Rent.ie for rentals
  • Budget for 1–2 months deposit upfront
  • Consider short-term lets while searching
  • Check commute times carefully
  • Join our community for housing tips

Month 2–3: Career

  • Localise your CV for Irish employers
  • Register with Irish recruitment agencies
  • Attend industry networking events
  • Get qualifications recognised (NARIC)
  • Join our live Q&A calls with recruiters

FAQ — Ireland

Common Questions About Moving to Ireland

Most skilled migrants need either a Critical Skills Employment Permit (for high-demand roles earning €38,000+) or a General Employment Permit (for other roles earning €34,000+). Your employer typically applies on your behalf through the Employment Permits Online System (EPOS). Our partner immigration advisors can guide you through the specific requirements for your situation.
Critical Skills Employment Permits typically take 4–8 weeks to process. General Employment Permits can take 8–16 weeks. Processing times can vary depending on application volume. It's important to apply well in advance of your intended start date.
Yes. Critical Skills Employment Permit holders can bring their spouse/partner and dependent children immediately. General Employment Permit holders must wait 12 months before applying for family reunification. Spouses of Critical Skills holders are entitled to work without a permit.
Ireland uses a PAYE (Pay As You Earn) system. Income tax rates are 20% on the first €42,000 and 40% above that. You'll also pay PRSI (social insurance, ~4%) and USC (Universal Social Charge, 0.5%–8% depending on income). Your employer deducts these automatically. You'll need a PPS Number to work legally.
Ireland, particularly Dublin, has a competitive rental market. It's advisable to arrange short-term accommodation (Airbnb, serviced apartments) for your first 2–4 weeks while you search for a permanent rental. Use Daft.ie and Rent.ie. Our community members regularly share tips and sometimes know of available properties before they're listed.
