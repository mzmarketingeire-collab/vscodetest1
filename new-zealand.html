Why New Zealand?

One of the World's Most Liveable Countries for Skilled Migrants

New Zealand consistently ranks among the world's top destinations for quality of life, safety, and natural beauty. With a skills shortage across construction, technology, and healthcare, the country is actively welcoming skilled migrants.

Richard, our NZ lead, lived in France for 14 years before returning to New Zealand. He knows first-hand what it takes to settle in a new country — and what it's like to come back and find things have changed.

  • 🏗️ Construction demand: Massive infrastructure programme and housing shortage driving demand for all construction trades
  • 💻 Tech growth: Auckland and Wellington tech scenes growing rapidly with strong startup culture
  • 🏥 Healthcare: Te Whatu Ora (Health NZ) actively recruiting internationally trained nurses and doctors
  • 🌿 Quality of life: Stunning nature, outdoor lifestyle, safe communities, and excellent schools
  • 🛂 Clear pathways: Structured visa system with clear routes to permanent residency and citizenship

Visa Pathways

New Zealand Visa Options for Skilled Workers

New Zealand has a clear, structured immigration system. Here's an overview of the main pathways. Note: This is informational only — consult our partner immigration advisors for personal advice.

⭐ Most Common

Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV)

The primary work visa for skilled migrants. Your employer must be accredited with Immigration New Zealand. Tied to your employer initially.

  • 🏢 Employer must be INZ accredited
  • 💰 Must meet median wage threshold (~NZD $29.66/hr)
  • ⏱️ Processing: 4–8 weeks
  • 📅 Valid up to 3 years
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Partner & children can join

Pathway to: Skilled Migrant Category Resident Visa

Skilled Migrant Category Resident Visa

The main pathway to permanent residency. Points-based system rewarding skilled employment, qualifications, and age.

  • 📊 Points-based — skilled job = 6 pts
  • 💼 Must have skilled employment in NZ
  • 🎓 NZ qualifications add points
  • 🏠 Leads to permanent residency
  • 🌍 Citizenship after 5 years

Best for: Those already working in NZ on AEWV

Other Pathways

Additional visa options depending on your situation, profession, or country of origin.

  • 🤝 Working Holiday Visa — explore NZ while working (age limits apply)
  • 🎓 Post-Study Work Visa — for NZ graduates
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Partnership Visa — for partners of NZ residents/citizens
  • 🏥 Health Workforce Visa — fast-track for healthcare workers

Job Market 2026

New Zealand's Hottest Sectors for Skilled Migrants

Where the demand is, what employers are paying, and how to get hired.

🏗️

Construction & Trades

New Zealand has a significant housing shortage and a major infrastructure programme. Project managers, engineers, quantity surveyors, electricians, and plumbers are all in high demand.

Salary Ranges (NZD, 2026)
Project Manager: $100,000–$150,000
Civil Engineer: $85,000–$130,000
Quantity Surveyor: $90,000–$135,000
Electrician: $70,000–$100,000
💻

Technology & IT

Auckland and Wellington have growing tech ecosystems. Software engineers, cloud architects, cybersecurity specialists, and data engineers are in strong demand from both local companies and multinationals.

Salary Ranges (NZD, 2026)
Software Engineer: $90,000–$140,000
Cloud Architect: $120,000–$170,000
Data Engineer: $100,000–$150,000
Cybersecurity: $110,000–$160,000
🏥

Healthcare

Te Whatu Ora (Health New Zealand) is actively recruiting internationally. Nurses, doctors, midwives, and allied health professionals are in high demand with dedicated immigration pathways.

Salary Ranges (NZD, 2026)
Registered Nurse: $65,000–$95,000
Senior Nurse: $90,000–$115,000
Physiotherapist: $70,000–$100,000
GP: $180,000–$280,000
Regions

Where to Live in New Zealand

New Zealand is geographically diverse. Each region offers a different lifestyle, job market, and cost of living.

🏙️

Auckland

NZ's largest city and economic hub. Most job opportunities, largest expat community, best international connections. Higher cost of living.

🏠 Avg rent (2-bed): NZD $2,800–$3,500/mo
✈️ International airport hub
💼 Largest job market
🏛️

Wellington

The capital city. Strong public sector, tech, and creative industries. Compact, walkable, and known for its café culture and arts scene.

🏠 Avg rent (2-bed): NZD $2,400–$3,000/mo
🏛️ Government & tech hub
🎭 Vibrant culture
🌿

Christchurch

The South Island's largest city. Ongoing rebuild creating massive construction demand. More affordable, great outdoor access, and a growing tech scene.

🏠 Avg rent (2-bed): NZD $2,000–$2,600/mo
🏗️ Construction boom
🏔️ Alpine lifestyle
🌊

Hamilton & Tauranga

Fast-growing regional cities. More affordable than Auckland, strong construction and agri-business sectors. Great lifestyle with beaches and outdoor activities.

🏠 Avg rent (2-bed): NZD $1,800–$2,400/mo
📈 Fast growing regions
💰 More affordable

Settling In

Your First 90 Days in New Zealand

A practical checklist for getting set up quickly and confidently in NZ.

📋

Week 1–2: Admin

  • ✅ Apply for IRD number (tax ID)
  • ✅ Open a bank account (ANZ, ASB, Kiwibank)
  • ✅ Register with a GP (doctor)
  • ✅ Get an NZ SIM card (Spark, One NZ, 2degrees)
  • ✅ Enrol in KiwiSaver (retirement savings)
🏠

Month 1: Housing & Transport

  • ✅ Use TradeMe Property and Realestate.co.nz
  • ✅ Budget for 4–6 weeks bond upfront
  • ✅ Consider buying a car early (essential outside Auckland)
  • ✅ Convert your overseas licence (within 12 months)
  • ✅ Join our community for housing tips
🎯

Month 2–3: Career & Community

  • ✅ Localise your CV for NZ employers
  • ✅ Get qualifications assessed (NZQA)
  • ✅ Register with professional bodies (Engineers NZ, NZNO)
  • ✅ Attend industry networking events
  • ✅ Join our live Q&A calls with NZ recruiters

FAQ — New Zealand

Common Questions About Moving to New Zealand

Most skilled migrants use the Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV). Your employer must be accredited with Immigration New Zealand before they can offer you a job under this visa. The process involves: employer gets accredited → job check → you apply for the visa. Our partner immigration advisors can guide you through each step.
The main pathway is the Skilled Migrant Category Resident Visa. It's a points-based system — having skilled employment in NZ is the key requirement (6 points). Additional points for NZ qualifications, age, and partner's employment. Most people apply after 12–24 months of working in NZ on an AEWV. Citizenship is available after 5 years of residency.
Outside of central Auckland and Wellington, a car is near-essential. Public transport is improving but limited in most areas. Budget for purchasing a reliable second-hand car in your first month — expect to spend NZD $8,000–$20,000 for a good used car. You can drive on your overseas licence for up to 12 months, then must convert to an NZ licence.
New Zealand uses a PAYE system. Tax rates range from 10.5% (up to NZD $14,000) to 39% (over NZD $180,000). You'll need an IRD number to work legally. KiwiSaver is a voluntary retirement savings scheme — most employers contribute 3% if you contribute 3%. New migrants get a 12-month exemption from KiwiSaver if they choose.
New Zealand, particularly Auckland, has become significantly more expensive in recent years. Housing is the biggest cost. However, salaries have also risen, and the quality of life — outdoor activities, safety, clean environment — is exceptional. Regional cities like Christchurch, Hamilton, and Tauranga offer a much better cost-to-lifestyle ratio than Auckland.
