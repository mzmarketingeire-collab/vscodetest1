👥 About Us

Built by People Who've
Lived the Experience

Settle In was founded by two people who know what it's like to move country, navigate a new system, and build a life from scratch. We built the resource we wish we'd had.

Our Mission

Relocation Shouldn't Be This Hard

Moving country is one of the biggest decisions a person can make. Yet most skilled migrants navigate it alone — piecing together information from outdated forums, unreliable websites, and well-meaning friends who moved years ago.

We built Settle In to change that. Our mission is to give skilled professionals the expert guidance, real community, and up-to-date intelligence they need to relocate with confidence — not confusion.

  • 🎯 Expert-led: Guides written by recruiters and professionals actively working in Ireland and NZ
  • 🤝 Community-first: Real connections with people on the same journey
  • 🔄 Always current: Monthly reports and regular guide updates keep information fresh
  • ⚖️ Honest: We tell you what it's really like — the good and the challenging
The Founders

Meet Mark & Richard

Two people who've lived the expat experience — from different angles, in different countries.

🍀 Ireland Lead

Mark

Irish recruiter. Expat in New Zealand. Construction & tech specialist.

Mark is from Ireland and has spent years recruiting in both the Irish technology sector and the New Zealand construction industry. He moved to New Zealand on a visa and has lived the experience that many of our members are going through right now.

He knows what Irish employers look for, how the Irish job market works, and what it's really like to land in a new country with a suitcase and a job offer. He leads the Ireland side of Settle In — bringing recruiter-level insight to every guide, Q&A call, and coaching session.

🥝 New Zealand Lead

Richard

Kiwi. 14 years in France. French citizen. Returned to NZ and rebuilt his life.

Richard is a New Zealander who moved to France, lived there for 14 years, and obtained French citizenship. He knows what it's like to be a foreigner in a new country — navigating a different language, culture, bureaucracy, and job market.

When he returned to New Zealand, he faced a different challenge: coming back to a country that had changed, and having to re-establish himself. That experience gave him a unique perspective on what returning migrants and new arrivals face in NZ. He leads the New Zealand side of Settle In — bringing both insider knowledge and genuine empathy to every interaction.
